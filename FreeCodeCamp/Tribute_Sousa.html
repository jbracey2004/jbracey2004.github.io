Tribute - John Philip Sousa

The American March King
John Philip Sousa, in 1900

Here are some events along Sousa's timeline
Date Event Info
November 6, 1854 John Philip Sousa is Born Washington D.C, U.S
Father: John Anthony Sousa
Mother: Maria Elisabeth Trinkhaus
1860 Begins Early music education Student of John Esputa and George Felix Benkert
Studied voice, violin, piano, flute, cornet, baritone, horn, trombone, and alto horn
He was also found to have perfect pitch.
June 6, 1867 Enlisted in United States Marine Corps as music apprentice He almost joined a circus band, but this was averted
1871 Completes honerably, his apprenticeship and first term in the U.S Marine Band He enlist for his second term in the Marines July 8, 1872
1873 Early Compositions copyrights "The Review March" and "The Cuckoo Galop"
1874 Completes honerably, his second term in the U.S Marine Band
Continues music career Studies Composition
Conducts Opera House in Washington D.C
Conducts Road Company in Matt Morgan's "Living Pictures"
First Chair as violintist at Centennial Exposition in Philadelphia
Plays in Mrs. Drew's Arch Street Theater Orchestra in Philadelphia
1875 - 1879 Directing and Conducting Conduccts road company in "The Poenix"
Chestnum Street Theater Orchestra in Philadelphia
Gorman's Philadelphia Church Choir Company in H.M.S. Pinafore
December 30, 1879 Marries Jane van Middlesworth
1880 Enlists in the Marines for a third term Leader and principal muscician
1880 - 1892 Serves as conductor of "The President's Own" in the U.S Marine Band under many presidents Rutherford B. Hayes from 1880 to 1881
James A. Garfield from March 1881 to September 1881
Chester A. Arthur from 1881 to 1885
Grover Cleveland from 1885 to 1889
Benjamin Harrison from 1889 to 1892
1892 Completes third term of the U.S Marine Crops July 30
New Marine Band in Plainfield, New Jersey September 26 		Over 15,000 concerts up until 1931
May 1893 World's Columbain Exposition
1901 Tours Great Britain
December 24, 1910 Begins World Tour Completes world tour at the New York Hippodrome, December 10, 2911
May 1915 Panama Pacific Exposition in San Franscisco, California
1917 Enlists in U.S. Navy
1922 Tours Cuba
May 6, 1929 Begins broadcasting on NBC
June 1930 Performs in "The Royal Welch Fusiliers March"
October 29, 1931 Completes last published march "The Circumnavigators Club"
March 6, 1932 Sousa dies Age 77
Reading, Pennslyvania
This was after a rehersal of Ringgold Band, conducting the "The Stars and Stripes Forever"
Buried in Congressional Cemetary in Washington D.C. March 10.
